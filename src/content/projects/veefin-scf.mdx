## 01 · The problem

Three banks, one product. Each has its own batch format, IFSC quirks, settlement windows and compliance rules. The platform has to look like a single calm SaaS on top of that chaos.

## 02 · What I own

- Payment and disbursement workflows across SBI, IDFC and Yes Bank rails.
- IDFC batch-ID generation with idempotent retries and audit trail.
- Automated financial operations: interest accrual, fee posting, repayment cycles.
- IFSC validation pipeline that catches bad data before it costs anyone money.
- Production triage — 160+ issues closed, most of them late on Thursday nights.

## 03 · Architecture, in plain English

- **Bank adapters.** Each bank has its own module. They all implement one interface.
- **A queue for everything.** RabbitMQ workers handle disbursements, so a bank outage doesn't bring down the UI.
- **Event-sourced ledger.** Every rupee has a story you can replay.
- **Redis, used carefully.** Sessions and a thin read-through cache. No caching of balances.

interface BankAdapter {
